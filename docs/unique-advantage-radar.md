# Unique Advantage Radar Pack

## Purpose

The Unique Advantage Radar is a local strategic discipline for evaluating whether a proposed mission strengthens Agent Trust Gate as future agent-to-agent trust infrastructure. It scores human-supplied feature assessments and records no live market data.

Project 3 should not compete as a generic AI governance platform. Project 3 should focus on agent-to-agent clearing, RefusalGraph refusal intelligence, safe receipts, and future fee-ready verification.

## Why Project 3 Needs An Advantage Radar

Useful engineering can still move a product toward a crowded category, increase complexity without creating customer value, or imitate features that larger companies can bundle. A repeatable review forces each mission to state why agents would use it, why machines might pay for it, what it adds to RefusalGraph or clearing, and what safety cost it creates.

The radar is not proof of demand or a substitute for customer evidence. It provides a consistent local hypothesis score that Gareth can challenge before authorizing work.

## What Makes A Feature 10/10

A ten-point feature is rare, valuable, defensible, simple enough to adopt, useful to autonomous systems, compatible with privacy boundaries, and connected to a clear future fee or commercial outcome. It improves the whole clearing loop rather than adding an isolated dashboard.

A feature only improves the project if it increases uniqueness, defensibility, agent usefulness, private-data safety, or future commercial potential.

## What Does Not Count As Advantage

- generic governance checklists already common in the market
- cosmetic breadth without agent or machine value
- a feature that depends on collecting private customer data
- speculative automation without a buyer, user, or fee trigger
- integrations that a platform owner can reproduce trivially
- scoring unsupported by technical validation or demand evidence
- live scanning, scraping, tracking, outreach, or publishing disguised as research
- payment or automatic purchase activation before governance and approval

## Unique Advantage Pillars

The radar evaluates uniqueness, defensibility, agent-to-agent value, machine-to-machine fee potential, RefusalGraph value, private-data safety, developer adoption, commercial clarity, resistance to bundling by large companies, and simplicity.

Strong missions reinforce multiple pillars. A high score in one dimension cannot hide severe privacy, complexity, adoption, or commercial weaknesses because the overall score is a transparent simple average.

## RefusalGraph Advantage

RefusalGraph converts privacy-minimised blocked, refused, limited, approval-required, missing-evidence, identity-unclear, and high-risk patterns into negative trust intelligence. This differs from tools that record only approvals or policy documents.

Its potential moat comes from safe accumulated refusal patterns, consistent reason vocabularies, receipt provenance, and usefulness at the moment another agent decides whether to accept a transaction. More signals are valuable only if quality, privacy, correction, appeal, abuse resistance, and context remain controlled.

## Agent Clearing House Advantage

The Agent Clearing House links declarations, evidence, approval, RefusalGraph caution, decisions, and receipts before action. Communication protocols move messages and payment protocols move value; the clearing layer evaluates whether a proposed transaction should proceed, be limited, require stronger proof, or remain blocked.

The advantage depends on neutral integration, explainable outcomes, receipt integrity, low-friction developer adoption, and trust from both requesting and receiving systems.

## Machine-to-Machine Fee Readiness

Future fee-ready capabilities may include clearance checks, refusal lookups, receipt verification, policy evidence, and signed audit proofs. A strong feature makes the fee trigger machine-readable and valuable without activating payment.

No fee, tariff, payment provider, billing ledger, settlement system, automatic purchase, or paid endpoint exists in this pack. Fee potential is a planning score, not revenue or payment readiness.

## Private Data Avoidance

Refusal intelligence and receipts should use closed reason codes, pseudonymous references, evidence hashes, minimal retention, and strict output allowlists. Features that need raw customer, company, account, wallet, message, document, or endpoint data should score lower until a justified privacy design exists.

High private-data safety does not prove anonymity or compliance. Re-identification, retention, access, deletion, correction, legal basis, and appeal require separate review.

## Defensibility And Moat Potential

Potential defensibility can come from trusted receipt semantics, refusal-data quality, integration standards, network learning without private-data leakage, switching costs created by useful audit history, and a reputation for neutral safety controls.

Code alone is not a moat. Large competitors can copy visible features. The radar therefore asks whether a mission strengthens data advantage, trust, standards position, workflow integration, or a commercial loop that remains difficult to reproduce responsibly.

## Feature Scoring Method

Each of ten dimensions receives a human-supplied score from zero to ten. Scores are clamped, averaged equally, and rounded to two decimals. The result maps to weak, ordinary, promising, strong, or exceptional bands and a corresponding recommendation.

The [Advantage Scoring Model](advantage-scoring-model.md) defines boundaries. Scorers must document assumptions and avoid treating a high score as authorization to build, launch, scan, charge, or connect externally.

## Future Mission Review Checklist

- define the exact agent or machine problem
- explain why existing governance tools do not solve it
- identify the RefusalGraph or clearing contribution
- state the machine-readable value and potential fee trigger
- assess private-data and live-activation risk
- identify overlap with large platforms and likely copying paths
- score all ten dimensions with evidence and uncertainty
- preserve required safety blocks
- choose reject, improve, safe build, high priority, or strategic priority
- obtain Gareth final approval for any live or commercial step

Use the [Future Mission Advantage Review Template](future-mission-advantage-review-template.md) for each mission.

## Current Status

The radar is draft-only, local-only, non-live, and non-commercial. Inputs are human-supplied local scores. The scorer performs arithmetic only and does not validate market claims, demand, competitors, legality, compliance, technical feasibility, or revenue.

## Safety Blocks

Nothing in the radar activates live scanning, outreach, tracking, billing, payment, deployment, or network use. Market scanning, competitor scraping, external lookup, public APIs, analytics, tracking, outreach, publishing, deployment, signup, billing, payment, settlement, machine fees, webhooks, third-party connections, live customer data, private-data export, automatic purchase, and action execution remain disabled.

## Gareth Final Approval Gate

Gareth final approval is required before any commercial or live activation. Approval must follow technical validation, security and privacy review, legal review, and commercial validation.

The current decision is **BLOCKED: LOCAL STRATEGIC SCORING ONLY**. The radar may compare human-supplied local hypotheses; it must not collect, publish, contact, track, charge, connect, deploy, or execute.
